  • When Facebook rebranded its corporate identity to Meta in October 2021 and announced plans to invest at least $10 billion or more in the concept in 2021, the term became a household word. In addition to Meta, tech giants like Google, Microsoft, Nvidia and Qualcomm also announced that they are planning to invest billions of dollars into the concept too. But what on earth are the metaverse?

    The current metaverse is a vision of what many in the computer and technology industry believe is the next generation of the Internet, Web 3.0, which is a single shared immersive persistent 3D virtual space where humans could experience lift in more ways than one that they couldn’t in the real and physical world.

    Many technologies will be used to provide access to the metaverse such as virtual reality (VR) headsets, augmented reality (AR) glasses and adequate bandwidth.

    Metaverse economy is predicted to reach $5 trillion by 2030. E-commerce is expected to be the dominant industry and engine of the metaverse. Gaming, entertainment, education and marketing will also become the important sectors of the entire metaverse.

  • The Japanese attack into Pearl Harbor is the most important reason leading to the by-then neutral country of the United States of America entering the war on the side of the allies.

    Japan attacked Pearl Harbour for many reasons. The Japanese attempted to attack Pearl Harbor as a preventive action to prevent the United States of America Pacific Fleet from interfering with its own planned military actions in Southeast Asia against overseas territories of the United Kingdom, the Netherlands and those of the United States of America.

    However, the Japanese attack was also driven by its own political self-interests in the Southeast Asia region, the scarcity of their own economic resources and perceived opportunity costs and United States embargo policy on Japan that cut off Japan access to US oil.

  • The Cold War was a term commonly used to refer to a period of geopolitical tension between the United States and the Soviet Union, with their respective allies, the Western Bloc and the Eastern Bloc. It lasted from May 12 1947 to December 26 1991.

    The Cold War occurred at many locations including the United States of America, Soviet Union, Europe, Cuba, Vietnam, North and South Korea, Greece, South America and East Asia.

    The Cold War broke out when during World War 2 (WW2) which transformed the United States and the USSR into world power, competition between both of them arised and after the defeat of the Axis power, an ideological and political rivalry between the United States and USSR gave way into the start of the Cold War.
